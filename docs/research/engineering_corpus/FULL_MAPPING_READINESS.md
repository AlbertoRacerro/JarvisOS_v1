# Full-Corpus Mapping Readiness — v2.2 Rehearsal

## Verdict

**HOLD — do not start unattended mapping of the full 11,000+ page corpus yet.**

The v2.2 representation is structurally useful, but the 150-record production rehearsal did not justify a full automatic rollout.

## Evidence

- 150 candidate microtopics compiled from 17 sources;
- 150/150 satisfy the strict JSON Schema;
- 950/951 embedded structural and semantic checks passed;
- 39/44 independent content checks passed;
- five source-artifact checks failed;
- one candidate has unresolved mass-transfer coefficient units/bases;
- 37/150 records satisfy the strict canonical-export gate;
- 113/150 remain withheld;
- the genuinely independent-reviewer gate was not run.

Schema validity is high, while verified knowledge yield is only **24.7%**.

## Interpretation

The low export yield is not a reason to weaken the gate. It shows that extraction is cheaper than verification.

The next bounded slice should prioritize:

1. better source-region selection;
2. typed correlation and coefficient-basis verification;
3. spreadsheet cell lineage and independent recalculation;
4. graphical/table extraction with interpolation uncertainty;
5. FEM target and convergence contracts;
6. independent domain review;
7. promotion of only verified canonical records.

## Next rehearsal

Run a verification-focused batch over 50–75 currently withheld records. Require deterministic recomputation for every numerical or equation-bearing record and one genuinely independent reviewer when available. Target at least 70% strict export yield without weakening any gate.

If the yield remains low, revise the extraction/review workflow rather than expanding to the full corpus.

## Benchmark boundary

Benchmark design may start only from strictly exported canonical records after a separate benchmark review. Withheld records and failed source-audit fragments are excluded from benchmark gold.
